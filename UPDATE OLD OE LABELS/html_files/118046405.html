By the Court:-

This petition is filed against demand of Rs.1,38,704/- which, according to respondent Jaipur Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited, is outstanding to petitioner in electricity bills. The bill pertained to the year 1983.

Contention of learned counsel for petitioner is that demand has been raised without any notice. Respondents have no authority to attach petitioner's property under Section 229 of the Land Revenue Act, 1956.

Billing dispute cannot be directly agitated entertained in extraordinary scope of writ jurisdiction. Petitioner, however, may make a representation to respondent no.2 - the Recovery Officer-cum-Executive Engineer (O&M), Jaipur Discom, Tonk, who shall consider the same and pass speaking order within four weeks thereon from the date it is made. If petitioner is still aggrieved by decision on his representation, he may approach the District Consumer Protection Forum or file civil suit as may be advised to him.

Writ petition is accordingly disposed of. This also disposes of stay application.

(Mohammad Rafiq) J.
